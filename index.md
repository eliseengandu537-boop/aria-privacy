# Privacy Policy for Aria

**Last updated: 2026-05-26**

This Privacy Policy explains how Aria ("we", "us", or "the app") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our mobile application.

By using Aria, you agree to the practices described below.

---

## 1. Information We Collect

We collect only the information needed to make Aria work:

### Account information
- **Email address and password** when you sign up. Passwords are hashed by Firebase Authentication; we never see your password in plain text.

### Reminders and profile
- The **reminders, tasks, notes, due dates, priorities, categories, and favorites** you create.
- Profile information you provide during onboarding: **name, role, schedule, goals, habits, and preferred tone**. You can edit or remove this anytime in Settings.

### Optional integrations (you opt in)
- **Phone calendar**: with your permission, we read events from your device's calendar to show them alongside your reminders. We never share or upload your calendar.
- **Gmail account**: if you connect Gmail, we receive an authorization token that lets the app send emails on your behalf at the times you schedule. We only use the `gmail.send` scope. We do not read, store, or transmit any of your other emails.
- **WhatsApp**: Aria only prepares messages and opens WhatsApp with them prefilled. We do not access WhatsApp data, contacts, or message history.

### AI conversations
- Messages you type into the AI Assistant, together with your profile and open reminders, are sent to Google Gemini to generate replies. See "Third-party services" below.

### Technical information
- Anonymous crash and performance reports from Google's standard mobile services. No personal content is included.

---

## 2. How We Use Your Information

- To create and sync your reminders across your devices.
- To send notifications and emails at the times you schedule.
- To personalize the AI Assistant's responses.
- To improve app stability and fix bugs.

We do **not** sell your data to anyone. We do not show targeted ads.

---

## 3. Where Your Data Is Stored

- Reminders, profile, and account data are stored in **Google Firebase** (Authentication and Cloud Firestore), governed by Google's security and privacy practices.
- Your data is only readable by you. Firestore security rules restrict access so that no other user — and no employee or third party — can read your reminders.
- Gmail authorization tokens are stored locally on your device; we do not transmit them to our servers.

---

## 4. Third-Party Services

Aria relies on the following Google services:

| Service | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Firebase Authentication | Sign-in |
| Cloud Firestore | Store and sync your reminders |
| Google Gemini API | Power the AI Assistant |
| Google Sign-In + Gmail API | Send scheduled emails on your behalf |

These services are governed by Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

---

## 5. Your Rights

You can at any time:

- **Edit or delete** any reminder, your profile, or your Gmail connection in the app.
- **Disconnect Gmail** in Settings to revoke the app's email-send permission.
- **Delete your account**: contact us at the email below and we will permanently remove your account and all associated data within 30 days.
- **Request a copy of your data**: contact us at the email below.

You can also revoke Gmail access at any time from your Google account: https://myaccount.google.com/permissions

---

## 6. Children's Privacy

Aria is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 13. If you believe we have, contact us and we will delete the data.

---

## 7. Data Retention

We keep your data only as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, we remove your data within 30 days.

---

## 8. Security

We use industry-standard security practices:
- Passwords hashed by Firebase Authentication.
- All data transmitted over HTTPS.
- Firestore security rules restrict access to only the account owner.

No system is 100% secure. We do our best, but we cannot guarantee absolute security.
